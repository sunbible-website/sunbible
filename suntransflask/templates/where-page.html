BENIN

Mission Évangélique des Sourds

The Benin Sustainable Development Plan hosts two different sessions each week on Wednesday and Saturdays at Vedoko Deaf Church in Cotonou. Thirty-four people are attending, including twelve new students of SUN.

Grace Ballo, the team leader, reports, the review of the thirteen lessons is underway, and the team is super motivated. Participants are really enthusiastic, seeing the program as a personally and spiritually enriching opportunity.

It was a miracle and a revival in the church because most of them participated in the SUN program and understood the Scriptures through SUN. We bless the Lord for the SUN program initiated by Wycliffe Associates.

Grace Ballo, SUN trainer, Cotonou, Benin

BURKINA FASO

Federation of Evangelical Churches and Missions (FEME) with the Assemblies of God Church

Ramde Fadirou, a new convert, shared that he was touched after reading Ephesians 1:4 in SUN, (In Him God chose us before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and blameless before Him). This was new information for him.

BURUNDI

Immanuel Church of the Deaf

Cibitoke, Burundi training ended last December, since then fifteen SUN facilitators organized local trainings in four different municipalities reaching 123 new Deaf students with the program.

The SUN leader reports, After learning SUN, the beneficiaries have just set up a followship program in their area on Fridays of every week to learn a lot about the word of God, popularize it, and improve their knowledge about the SUN program. eleven Deaf people who were pagans and did not belong to any religious denomination became Christians; 100% of the beneficiaries do not know how to read the written Bible, they did not know the divine word but for the moment they have a notion of the word of God.

COTE D’IVOIRE

Eglise Du Christ Internationale CI

Former participants of one event from Cote d’Ivoire continue to read the book of Mark with the SUN symbols in their homes.

The facilitators assist them and bring them together in groups of two, three, four etc... according to their respective localities.

Other facilitators find classrooms on holidays to bring together some former deaf-mutes to review the symbols.

"We are thinking of printing other books of the New Testament to make them available so that they can continue to read the Bible with the symbols."

NIGERIA

Voice for the Deaf Foundation

Nigeria Sustainable Development Plan in seven locations including Deaf churches and classrooms. The Deaf churches are growing in number.

The SUN leader reports, The churches initially have quite a number of members. But the far distance of the church from the homes of the members discourages people from attending church services. However, through the help and Grace of God and SUN, the church has been experiencing growth to the Glory of God Almighty. This is because the members are enthusiastic towards knowing more about SUN, being a new style of language to them. We thank God for everything.
